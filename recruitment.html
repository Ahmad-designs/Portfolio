Project Overview

A comprehensive hiring platform tailored specifically for recruiters in the Gulf region. The goal of this project was to design a seamless and efficient experience for recruiters to find, evaluate, and hire candidates with ease. The platform enables recruiters to filter through candidates, manage orders, track applications, and personalize their hiring dashboard.

Problem Statement

" Recruiters in the Gulf face challenges when hiring candidates from India due to poor communication channels, lack of post-hiring tracking, and mismatches in candidate selection, leading to inefficiencies and hiring errors."

Design Goals

  • Efficient Filtering: Enable recruiters to find the right candidate with minimum effort.
  • Streamlined Workflow: Reduce the steps required to shortlist, order, or download resumes.
  • Dashboard Utility: Provide a functional, personalized dashboard for recruiters to track and manage hiring processes.

Key Features

1. Candidate List Page
  • Compact and card-based layout for easy scanning
  • Smart filters (experience, skills, nationality, availability, etc.)
  • Action buttons (Add to Wishlist, Order, View Profile)
2. Candidate Details Page
  • Full candidate profile: education, experience, skills, languages, certifications, availability
  • Buttons for easy actions (Add to Wishlist, Order, Download Resume in PDF )
  • Visual indicators (availability, rating, etc.)
3. Recruiter Dashboard
  • Personalized dashboard after login
  • Overview of recent orders and Wishlist activity
  • Order tracking with real-time updates
  • Profile settings: personal info, password settings, notification preferences etc.

UX Process

1. Research & Discovery

To gain a deep understanding of the needs, pain points, and expectations of our users, we conducted a comprehensive research phase comprising both qualitative and quantitative methods.

We interviewed 10 recruiters from different Gulf countries, including the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar. These recruiters came from diverse industries such as construction, healthcare, and hospitality.

Key Insights from Interviews:
  • Most recruiters rely heavily on manual processes or spreadsheets for tracking candidates.
  • Filters like nationality, visa status, and trade experience are critical when shortlisting.
  • Many recruiters felt overwhelmed by cluttered platforms that showed too much irrelevant information.

We distributed a survey to 20 Gulf-based recruitment firms. 76% of respondents said they often face difficulties in finding matching candidates within a specific timeframe. 64% of recruiters expressed a strong need for centralized dashboards that combine order and candidate tracking.

We analyzed five existing platforms used by Gulf recruiters.

Key gaps identified:
  • Poor filtering mechanisms.
  • Limited candidate detail views.
  • No option to save or group preferred profiles.
  • Weak dashboards lacking order tracking.

4. Testing & Feedback

Stakeholder & Recruiter Testing Sessions.

  • Measured user engagement, click-through rates, and layout preferences
  • Tested CTAs, filters, and visual hierarchy

Outcome & Impact

  • Higher engagement on candidate Wishlist feature.
  • Positive feedback on order tracking and dashboard usability.
